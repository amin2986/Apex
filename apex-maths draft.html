Why It Works

Four Things That Make
the Real Difference

Most students aren't stuck because they're not clever enough. They're stuck because nobody has shown them the right approach. Here's what we do differently.

🎯

We Teach Exam Thinking, Not Just Content

There's a gap between knowing maths and earning marks in an exam. We close it. Students learn how examiners think — how to structure answers, where method marks live, and how to avoid the mistakes that silently cost grades every year.

→ Students stop losing marks they already know how to earn
📝

Real Past Papers. Written Feedback. Every Session.

Every session includes genuine past paper practice under exam conditions — not worksheets, not textbook exercises. Afterwards, every student receives written feedback on their specific errors. Not a class debrief. Their errors.

→ Students improve on the exact questions that cost them grades
📐

A Plan Built Around Your Child

Before anything else, every student gets a free diagnostic assessment. We map out where they're strong, where they're losing marks, and which topics offer the highest return on time invested. Then we build a plan around that — not a generic syllabus.

→ No time wasted on topics they already understand
🔄

Consistent, Structured Momentum

Two sessions per week isn't arbitrary. It's the cadence that builds real retention. Enough to maintain momentum and regularly revisit topics. Not so much that it becomes unsustainable alongside school. The programme is designed to work alongside Year 10 and 11 — not against it.

→ Steady, measurable progress term after term

FAQs

Questions Parents Ask

The most common things families want to know before joining. If yours isn't here, just get in touch.

Most students notice a tangible shift in confidence and approach within 3–4 weeks. Significant grade jumps — the kind visible in mock results — typically happen over one full term (8–12 weeks) of consistent attendance. Students who engage actively with the weekly past paper practice tend to progress fastest.
All Apex sessions are delivered fully online via live video — no travelling, no commuting, and no wasted time. Sessions run exactly as they would in person: live teaching, real-time feedback, and full interaction between students and the tutor. All you need is a laptop or tablet and a stable internet connection.
Sessions run across weekday evenings and weekends to fit around school timetables. Specific times are confirmed at booking based on group availability. We do our best to accommodate preferred days wherever possible.
We understand school life is busy. If a session is missed with reasonable notice, we'll do our best to arrange a catch-up or provide notes and materials from that session so your child doesn't fall behind.
No long-term contracts. We ask for a minimum commitment of one half-term (around 6 weeks) so students have enough time to genuinely benefit. After that, you continue term by term and can cancel with one month's notice.
Year 10 is actually the ideal time to start. Beginning early means more time to close gaps, build genuine confidence, and arrive in Year 11 already performing at a high level. Several of our biggest grade jumps started in Year 10.
All major GCSE Maths exam boards — Edexcel, AQA, and OCR. We tailor past paper practice and exam technique to match your child's specific board from the very first session.
Large tutoring centres often have 15–20+ students per class, rotating staff, and off-the-shelf materials. At Apex, we cap every group at a strict maximum of six students. Your child is never lost in the crowd — they get real attention, written feedback on their specific errors, and a tutor who knows exactly where they stand.
